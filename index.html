Camellia Sinensis / Ananas Comosus Fiber Based Uni-Directional Corrugated Panel for Sound Damping Application


Executed a groundbreaking project where innovation meets sustainability in the realm of sound damping technology. Our mission was to engineer an environmentally friendly sound damping panel tailored for higher frequency applications, revolutionizing the industry with a unique approach. By harnessing the natural fibers, we crafted a uni-directional corrugated panel structure embedded within epoxy resin. The plates developed in corrugated shape and molded through mold pressing method. In collaboration with esteemed mentor Gokul Kumar. S and dedicated peers Reavanth. S and Suryaprakash. A, we successfully patented this eco-friendly panel, marking a significant milestone in sustainable sound damping solutions.
Highlights:

  • The panel is made with Camellia sinensis (tea plant) and Ananas comosus (pineapple), added along with E-Glass Fibres to increase strength.
  • The panel was ingeniously designed featuring a sinusoidal corrugatedpattern sandwiched betweenflat fiber plates.
  • The NFRHCs (Natural Fibre Reinforced Hybrid Composites) are planned with a 5 mm thickness and 7 plies, each segmented into 0.71 mm parts

Requirement Specification for Water Distribution Network and design for Firefighting in a Large Ship


The primary goal of this project is to develop an efficient Water Distribution Network for the Firefighting System on a ship comprising 10 compartments. Our approach is rooted in a top-down methodology, where we meticulously identified system requirements and crafted a detailed architectural diagram. Through extensive research and expert consultations, I took personal ownership of designing the entire system architecture to align with customer specifications. With conducting rigorous analysis it enabled us to enhance the system's reliability in alignment with customer demands. Our commitment to a detailed and thoughtful design process we have verified and validated the design to ensures the Water Distribution Network for the Firefighting System meets customer expectations.
Highlights:

  • To ensure the system's resilience, we conducted a comprehensive Fault Tree Analysis to assess failure probabilities across various scenarios during the feasibility study.
